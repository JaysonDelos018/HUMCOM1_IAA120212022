How To Assemble A Computer Safely

Step 1: Open Case

  1. Remove the screws
  2. Take the side cover off

Tip:It is easier to work on your PC with it laying sideways on a flat surface

Step 2: Mount Motherboard

  1. Place the motherboard into the I/O plate
  2. Fasten the motherboard in place on top of the mounting standoffs

Tip:Install the mounting standoffs in the case positions that match the screw mounting holes on your motherboard

Step 3: Mount Processor (CPU)

  1. Locate the CPU socket holder on the motherboard
  2. Lift up the latch lever to release and hinge open the CPU socket holder
  3. Gently place the processor in the motherboard CPU socket holder
  4. Lower the latch lever and the hinge to close the CPU socket and hold the processor

Tip:Dont apply force when placing the processor to avoid damage

Step 4: Install CPU Cooler

  1. Apply thermal paste to the back of the processor
  2. Place the heatsink/cooler and fix its position
  3. Plug the power cable attached to the cooler fan into the motherboard connector

Tip:Dont apply too much thermal paste just the right ammount

Step 5: Install Power Supply Unit (PSU)

  1. Mount the power supply and fasten with screws to the case mounting points
  2. Plug the largest cabling connector from the supply cabling into the motherboard power connector
  3. Plug the 8-pin cabling conenctor from the power supply cabling into the CPU power connector

Tip:Dont misinterpret other cables that is not used in the power supply unit

Step 6: Mount Memory (RAM)

  1. Press to open the clips at both ends of the RAM slot
  2. Line up the notch on the RAM stick with the RAM slots
  3. Place and press the RAM. The tabs should automatically close as you press down the the RAM

Tip:Use the same RAM hertz to maximize its performance

Step 7: Install Graphics Cards

  1. Remove the expansion slot covers from the rear case where the graphics card will be placed
  2. Place the graphics card into the graphics card slot located at the lower half of the motherboard
  3. Put in the screws to hold the graphics cards into place
  4. Plug in the power connector cables from your power supply into the graphics card power connector

Step 8: Mount Storage Drivers

  1. Mount storage drives in the case drive bays
  2. Connect the drive to the motherboard using a SATA cable
  3. Plug in the power cabling to the storage drive
  4. Mount any other storage drives in the same way
      5. Storage Drive Sizes
      • 3.5" form factor
      • 2.5" form factor

Tip:2.5" factor drives may need an adaptor plate to mount them within your PC case due to their smaller size

Step 9: Connect Case Fans

  1. Mount any case fans within your case as required using the supplied screws
  2. Connect any case fan power conenctors to the multiple fan headers located at various places on the motherboard

Tip:Fans are important to cool your Unit to avoid overheats

Step 10: Close Case & Connect Peripherals

  1. Place the side cover back on
  2. Secure the side panel with case screws
  3. Connect peripheral devices
      4. Peripheral devices
      • mouse
      • keyboard
      • monitor
      • speakers
      • headsets
      • webcam
